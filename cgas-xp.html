The problem

CET's explosion-proof offering ran entirely on a white-label product. Despite 2,000+ quotes over three years, only ~68 units sold — a 3% conversion rate. The cause was structural: the white-label price point landed us at $3,949 in a market that expected $1,400–$2,100. We had no ability to differentiate on features, no margin to compete on price, and zero supply-chain control.

When the white-label supplier was acquired, lead times ballooned and supply uncertainty became a live business risk. The product and commercial model needed to be rebuilt at the root.

My role

Sole product owner and program lead. I owned everything: product scope, system architecture, certification strategy, NPV modeling, pricing, global component sourcing, channel strategy, and GTM execution. Budget approval was aligned with leadership — all analysis, inputs, and decisions were mine.

Discovery first

Before defining a single feature, I ran 10–15 structured customer interviews across three market segments — commercial-industrial (distilleries, fleet maintenance, paint booths), heavy industrial (wastewater, chemical processing, midstream oil & gas), and premium industrial.

The most important finding came up repeatedly and reshaped our positioning entirely:

Customers often spec explosion-proof not just for hazardous location classification, but for durability and "industrial look" — whether the device visually belongs in a harsh environment. This meant feature parity with competitors wasn't the goal. A platform designed to grow was.

Discovery also surfaced strong consensus on must-haves: remote calibration (considered mandatory — some customers switch to explosion-proof specifically to enable it), BACnet directly from the transmitter (virtually no competitor offered this), and remote sensor support. Bluetooth and cloud connectivity got consistent negative signals — we deliberately excluded both.

Product design

I designed a platform, not a single SKU. The architecture supports:

  • Up to 3 sensors (internal and/or remote) — reduces install cost, simplifies system design
  • Broad gas coverage: EC, IR, CAT, PID — wider than most competitors in our segment
  • Native BACnet and Modbus — a genuine differentiator in the commercial-industrial tier
  • Remote calibration — validated as mandatory by every interview participant
  • Built on existing CET firmware — simplifies service, training, and support for a field team that already knows the platform

The key tradeoff: cutting ATEX from V1

After researching three certification labs and conducting the customer interviews, I cut ATEX from the first version.

ATEX would have added $52K+ in certification costs, 14+ additional weeks, and ongoing ISO 9001 audit obligations — for minimal incremental demand in our Class I Division 1 target segment. Cutting it let us enter CSA/UL certification on schedule, protect the development budget, and build pipeline while still in certification.

This was a deliberate, researched decision — not a compromise forced by constraints.

Financial modeling

I built a full NPV model across 5 scenarios comparing white-label continuation vs. in-house production at different price and volume assumptions.

  • White-label cost: ~$1,364/unit. In-house cost: ~$362/unit — a ~71% reduction
  • Total development investment: $104,628 (PCB redesign, engineering, CSA/EMC)
  • Target price: $1,750, competitive with the RKI M2A XL benchmark identified in discovery
  • NPV positive by FY27 at existing volume; significantly better with projected conversion improvement

Go-to-market

I designed a three-tier market approach prioritizing fast wins through the existing dealer network before expanding:

  • Tier 1 (fast wins) — existing dealers with industrial arms; distilleries, fleet maintenance, boiler rooms
  • Tier 2 (strategic expansion) — wastewater, chemical processing, midstream oil & gas
  • Tier 3 (long-term) — offshore, premium industrial, global certification markets

Launch execution included AHR Expo roadshow participation, dealer briefing materials, sales training, and full technical documentation for the first customer meetings.

Outcomes

  • Replaced high-risk white-label dependency — CET now owns the platform
  • ~71% per-unit cost reduction restores pricing competitiveness and margin control
  • Entered CSA/UL certification testing with validated market demand and early pipeline
  • Projected 200% sales increase in Q1 post-launch
  • Positioned CET for expansion into industrial instrumentation and fire & life safety
