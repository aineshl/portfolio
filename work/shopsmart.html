The Problem

Amazon shopping is cognitively exhausting — too many tabs, contradictory reviews, and star ratings that don't account for sample size. A 4.3 with 40 reviews and a 4.3 with 14,000 reviews look identical. 100+ surveys confirmed: 72% spent too much time comparing products, 54% couldn't reliably interpret rating vs. review volume. Amazon had filters — not synthesis.

My Role

Sole PM, designer, and developer — user research, problem framing, MVP scoping, Figma prototyping, full-stack development, scoring algorithm design, AI integration, user testing, and iteration.

What I Built

  • ShopSmart Score — weighted algorithm: price, rating, and review volume as a reliability proxy. Trustworthiness of stars, not just stars.
  • Recommended pick with rationale — users wanted a verdict, not a comparison table
  • AI feature summary — BART model synthesizing product description into key differentiators
  • AI review summary — BART model across the 100 most recent reviews
  • Parallel processing — both products scraped simultaneously with a progress loading state

What 15+ Testers Told Me

Slow processing → Parallel scraping + dynamic loading state.

Opaque scores → Added explanation of weighting. Users don't trust unexplained numbers.

No recommendation → Added the recommended pick with rationale. Users wanted a verdict.

Visual trust → Full redesign pass. Aesthetic matters when you're asking users to trust an algorithm.

Outcomes

Shipped a working MVP, tested with 15+ users, iterated on 4 major feedback themes, and delivered a polished final product. Demonstrates the full PM loop: research → prototype → build → test → iterate → ship.
