The Problem

The company had no formal product innovation or prioritization process. Ideas came in through multiple disconnected channels — support calls, emails, customer conversations — with no consistent way to evaluate, prioritize, or track them. Engineering worked reactively, without visibility into why work was being done or how it connected to business goals.

As the organization scaled, this created compounding friction: misalignment between product planning and engineering capacity, repeated context-switching, and no traceability from customer signal to shipped outcome.

My Role

Product Manager — I owned the zero-to-one design and rollout of CET's full product innovation system: the Jira configuration, scoring framework, SOP, sprint KPI model, and stakeholder alignment required to get engineering, sales, and leadership using it consistently.

The Solution

  • Centralized intake — one Jira Product Ideas board pulling from a submission form, RMA data, support tickets, and direct PM observation
  • Objective prioritization — impact vs. effort scoring visible to all stakeholders. Every item has a score, status, and owner
  • Clear governance — a written SOP defining lifecycle states from NEW IDEA through RELEASED TO MARKET, with approval gates and role responsibilities
  • Execution integration — approved ideas flow into bi-weekly sprints with story point estimation and KPI tracking

The System in Practice

Outcomes

The sprint KPI framework drove a ~19% improvement in engineering throughput over 17 sprints — establishing a shared cadence that directly improved planning accuracy and delivery predictability.

  • Established the company's first structured product innovation process — zero to operational
  • Improved alignment across product, engineering, support, and leadership
  • 130+ ideas and 40+ bugs managed and prioritized across 5 product lines
  • SOP written and in use — system runs independently of any single person
  • Features shipped through this system have driven measurable revenue impact
