The problem

When I joined CET, there was no formal product innovation or prioritization process. Feature ideas came in through support calls. Bugs were reported over email. Customer feedback lived in someone's notes from a sales call. RMAs sat in a separate system. None of it connected to engineering.

As the company scaled, this created compounding problems: work was driven by whoever was loudest that week, engineering capacity was consumed reactively, and there was no visibility into why any given piece of work was actually being done.

My role

Product Manager — I owned the zero-to-one design and rollout of CET's full product innovation system. That included the Jira configuration, the scoring framework, the SOP, the sprint KPI model, and the stakeholder alignment required to get engineering, sales, and leadership actually using it consistently.

The solution

I designed the system around four pillars:

  • Centralized intake — one Jira Product Ideas board pulling from an internal submission form, RMA data, support tickets, and direct PM observation. One place for everything.
  • Objective prioritization — impact vs. effort scoring visible to all stakeholders. Every item has a score, a status, and an owner. Decisions are transparent and defensible.
  • Clear governance — a written SOP defining lifecycle states from NEW IDEA through RELEASED TO MARKET, with approval gates at each stage and explicit role responsibilities for PM, Engineering Lead, and Management.
  • Execution integration — approved ideas flow directly into bi-weekly sprints. I partnered with engineering leadership to introduce story point estimation and sprint KPIs (points committed vs. completed), giving us a shared language for capacity and predictability.

What this created

End-to-end traceability — for the first time, anyone at CET could see exactly why a piece of engineering work was in development, what customer signal triggered it, and where it was in the lifecycle.

The sprint KPI framework drove a ~19% improvement in engineering throughput over 17 sprints. One feature that moved through this system contributed approximately $400K in annual revenue. The system now actively tracks 100+ ideas and ongoing engineering initiatives.

  • Established CET's first structured product innovation process — zero to operational
  • Improved alignment across product, engineering, support, and leadership
  • Enabled faster movement from approved ideas into active development
  • SOP written and in use — system runs without constant PM intervention
